# S.E.A. Members & Associated Characters

Comprehensive profiles of all known members of the Society of Explorers and Adventurers and related characters.

## Core S.E.A. Members

### Harrison Hightower III
**Status**: Deceased (disappeared 1899)
**Occupation**: Collector, industrialist
**Membership**: Corrupt member of S.E.A.
**Notable Artifacts**: Shiriki Utundu (cursed idol)

Harrison Hightower III was a wealthy and corrupt member of S.E.A. who collected treasures and antiquities from around the world through questionable means. He owned the Hotel Hightower in New York City. In 1899, he stole the cursed idol Shiriki Utundu from the Mtundu tribe in the Congo Basin. On December 31, 1899, during a New Year's Eve party, he mocked the curse by extinguishing his cigar on the idol's head. At midnight, green lightning struck the hotel, and Hightower disappeared in the elevator amid screams. He was never seen again.

**Connections**:
- Related to George Hightower (possibly cousins or brothers)
- Photographed outside Temple of the Crystal Skull (Indiana Jones Adventure)
- Depicted in murals stealing from Raging Spirits temple
- His story appears at Mystic Manor where he's photographed with Lord Henry Mystic
- Books referencing his expeditions appear at Skipper Canteen

**Locations**: Tower of Terror (Tokyo DisneySea)

---

### Lord Henry Mystic
**Status**: Active
**Occupation**: Explorer, collector
**Membership**: Senior member of S.E.A.
**Companion**: Albert (monkey)

Lord Henry Mystic is a prominent explorer and collector who established Mystic Manor to house his extensive collection of artifacts from around the world. He rescued his monkey companion Albert from a giant spider during his travels, and they became inseparable partners. Lord Henry acquired an enchanted music box that, when opened by Albert, brings his entire collection to life.

He expelled Professor Garrett Reed from S.E.A. for using questionable tactics in obtaining archaeological artifacts.

**Connections**:
- Photographed with Harrison Hightower III
- Expelled Professor Garrett Reed from S.E.A.
- Referenced in books at Skipper Canteen
- Portrait appears in Disney Cruise Line Oceaneer Lab

**Locations**: Mystic Manor (Hong Kong Disneyland)

---

### Barnabas T. Bullion
**Status**: Historical figure
**Occupation**: Mining magnate, businessman
**Membership**: S.E.A. member
**Character Inspiration**: Modeled after Disney Imagineer Tony Baxter

In 1848, Barnabas T. Bullion received a land grant to establish the Big Thunder Mining Company. He saw it as his destiny to acquire all the gold from Big Thunder Mountain. He sought help from his friend Jason Chandler (president of S.E.A.), who contacted Madame Zarkov from the Museum of the Weird. Zarkov warned Bullion to stop mining operations due to the Thunderbird curse, but Bullion continued despite the warnings.

**Connections**:
- Close friend of Jason Chandler
- Warned by Madame Zarkov about supernatural dangers
- Associated with Henry Ravenswood (who oversaw his mines at Thunder Mesa in the Paris version)

**Locations**: Big Thunder Mountain Railroad (Disneyland, Magic Kingdom, Disneyland Paris)

---

### Captain Mary Oceaneer
**Status**: Active
**Occupation**: Sea captain, treasure hunter
**Membership**: S.E.A. member
**Companion**: Duncan (parrot), Salty (parrot)

Captain Mary Oceaneer is a world-famous treasure hunter and sea captain. She made it her goal to sail the seven seas and collect treasures to share with the world. On December 5, 1901, she discovered a golden sword during a deep sea dive, accompanied by her parrot Salty wearing a custom diving suit. During one expedition, a rogue storm struck while she was diving for treasure, carrying her ship and precious artifacts to what would become Typhoon Lagoon. Her current diving partner is her parrot Duncan, who keeps watch and sings while she searches for treasure.

**Connections**:
- The RV Oceaneer Lab (Disney Cruise ships) is her first ship
- Her artifacts and letters appear in Disney Cruise Line Oceaneer Labs
- References to other S.E.A. members (Henry Mystic, Harrison Hightower III) appear in her collection

**Locations**: Miss Adventure Falls (Typhoon Lagoon), Disney Cruise Line Oceaneer Lab

---

### Jason Chandler
**Status**: Historical figure
**Occupation**: Inventor, explorer
**Membership**: Club Secretary (1851), President (c. 1880)

Jason Chandler was a 19th-century inventor who served as club secretary under President Vitale Robustelli in 1851. By around 1880, he was elected president of S.E.A. In 1882, he embarked on a riverboat expedition in British Columbia. After 1907, he and French aviator Captain Brieux mapped regions of the Mekong River from Brieux's airship, The Hyperion.

He was a close friend of Barnabas T. Bullion and contacted Madame Zarkov to warn Bullion about supernatural dangers at Big Thunder Mountain.

**Connections**:
- Close friend of Barnabas T. Bullion
- Worked with Captain Brieux
- Consulted with Madame Zarkov
- Created for the unbuilt Discovery Bay concept but incorporated into S.E.A. lore

**Locations**: Big Thunder Mountain Railroad references, original Discovery Bay concept

---

### Dr. Albert Falls
**Status**: Missing
**Occupation**: Explorer, founder of Jungle Navigation Company
**Membership**: S.E.A. member
**Family**: Grandfather of Alberta Falls

Dr. Albert Falls was a prominent explorer and S.E.A. member who founded the Jungle Navigation Company. He mysteriously disappeared years before his granddaughter Alberta took over the company. His legacy lives on through the Skipper Canteen restaurant at Magic Kingdom, which features books written by various S.E.A. members.

**Connections**:
- Grandfather of Alberta Falls
- Friend of Dr. Kon Chunosuke
- The Skipper Canteen features his legacy

**Locations**: Jungle Skipper Canteen (Magic Kingdom), Jungle Cruise

---

### Alberta Falls
**Status**: Active
**Occupation**: Owner/manager of Jungle Navigation Company, bookkeeper, head mechanic
**Membership**: Not confirmed as S.E.A. member (carries on grandfather's legacy)
**Family**: Granddaughter of Dr. Albert Falls, distant cousin to Siobhan "Puffin" Murphy

Alberta Falls inherited the Jungle Navigation Company following her grandfather Dr. Albert Falls' mysterious disappearance. In 1938, she met Dr. Kon Chunosuke when she recognized the S.E.A. logo on his supplies and invited him on a jungle cruise. The expedition went awry when skipper Felix Pechman XIII crashed the boat, stranding them in the African veldt where they were chased up a pole by a rhinoceros.

**Connections**:
- Granddaughter of Dr. Albert Falls
- Met Dr. Kon Chunosuke in 1938
- Distant cousin to Siobhan "Puffin" Murphy

**Locations**: Jungle Cruise (Disneyland, Magic Kingdom) - 2021 update

---

### Camellia Falco
**Status**: Historical figure
**Occupation**: Aviation pioneer, inventor
**Membership**: First female S.E.A. member (1851)

Camellia Falco was an aviation pioneer from the early 1800s who became the first female member of S.E.A., officially brought in by President Vitale Robustelli in 1851. She developed the Dream Flyer, a glider inspired by Leonardo da Vinci's flying machine designs. The Museum of Fantastic Flight in Porto Paradiso presents a retrospective on her life. Her spirit appears to guide guests on the Dream Flyer for an imaginative flight around the world.

**Connections**:
- Inducted by Vitale Robustelli
- Inspired by Leonardo da Vinci's designs

**Locations**: Soaring: Fantastic Flight (Tokyo DisneySea)

---

### Vitale Robustelli
**Status**: Historical figure
**Occupation**: S.E.A. President (1851)
**Membership**: S.E.A. President

Vitale Robustelli served as president of the Society of Explorers and Adventurers in 1851. During his tenure, he made the historic decision to induct Camellia Falco as the first female member of the society, with Jason Chandler serving as club secretary under him.

**Connections**:
- Worked with Jason Chandler
- Inducted Camellia Falco

**Locations**: Referenced in Fortress Explorations history

---

### Merriweather Adam Pleasure
**Status**: Historical figure
**Occupation**: Explorer, adventurer, founder of Adventurers Club
**Membership**: S.E.A. member with personal chapter

Merriweather Adam Pleasure was the founder of Pleasure Island (fictional backstory) and established the Adventurers Club in 1921 to house his collection and entertain fellow explorers and adventurers. His club served as his personal S.E.A. chapter. Evidence of his S.E.A. membership includes a fez housed in the Skipper Canteen at Magic Kingdom.

**Connections**:
- Founded his own S.E.A. chapter at the Adventurers Club

**Locations**: Adventurers Club (defunct - Walt Disney World Pleasure Island), referenced at Skipper Canteen

---

### Jock Lindsay
**Status**: Active
**Occupation**: Pilot, seaplane tour operator
**Membership**: S.E.A. member or closely connected
**Notable Association**: Indiana Jones' pilot

Jock Lindsay was Indiana Jones' trusted pilot. In 1938, the two stumbled upon Disney Springs while chasing a mythology-based tip in central Florida. Attracted to the natural springs and lush terrain, Jock bought waterfront property and eventually settled down, piloting seaplane tours across Florida. He built an airplane hangar, air tower, and runway, and invited fellow S.E.A. members to visit. His hangar bar is packed with artifacts and references tying into both Indiana Jones stories and the larger S.E.A. universe.

**Connections**:
- Indiana Jones' pilot
- Hosted S.E.A. members at his hangar

**Locations**: Jock Lindsey's Hangar Bar (Disney Springs)

---

### Dr. Kon Chunosuke
**Status**: Active (1930s)
**Occupation**: Entomologist
**Membership**: S.E.A. member
**Nationality**: Japanese

Dr. Kon Chunosuke was a Japanese entomologist during the 1930s who traveled to the jungle seeking new insect specimens for his world-class collection. In 1938, he met Alberta Falls, who recognized the S.E.A. logo on his equipment and invited him on a jungle cruise. The expedition became stranded when skipper Felix Pechman XIII crashed their boat, and they were chased up a pole by a rhinoceros in the African veldt. A crate of books addressed to him from Mystic Point appears in the Jungle Cruise queue.

**Connections**:
- Friend of Dr. Albert Falls (through granddaughter Alberta)
- Received books from Mystic Point
- Expedition companion to Alberta Falls

**Locations**: Jungle Cruise (2021 update)

---

### Professor Garrett Reed
**Status**: Expelled from S.E.A.
**Occupation**: Archaeologist
**Membership**: Former S.E.A. member (expelled)

Professor Garrett Reed was expelled from S.E.A. by Lord Henry Mystic for using questionable tactics to obtain archaeological artifacts. After his expulsion, he sought the Emerald Trinity. His story is featured in the Curse of the Emerald Trinity overlay for Hong Kong Disneyland's Jungle Cruise.

**Connections**:
- Expelled by Lord Henry Mystic
- Rival to S.E.A. members after expulsion

**Locations**: Jungle Cruise - Curse of the Emerald Trinity (Hong Kong Disneyland)

---

### Captain Brieux

**Occupation**: French aviator
**Membership**: S.E.A. member

Captain Brieux was a French aviator and S.E.A. member. Following 1907, he and Jason Chandler mapped regions of the Mekong River together from Brieux's airship, The Hyperion.

**Connections**:
- Expedition partner of Jason Chandler

## Associated Characters (Not S.E.A. Members)

### George Hightower
**Status**: Deceased (murdered 1877)
**Occupation**: Wealthy world-traveler
**Origin**: Newport Beach, California

George Hightower was a wealthy world-traveler and relative of Harrison Hightower III (exact relation unknown, but likely cousins or brothers). In 1877, he married Constance Hatchaway, the infamous "Black Widow Bride." They moved to an antebellum mansion in New Orleans, Louisiana. Constance murdered George with an axe blow to the skull and severed his head, inheriting his estate.

**Connections**:
- Related to Harrison Hightower III
- Husband (and victim) of Constance Hatchaway

**Locations**: Haunted Mansion lore

---

### Constance Hatchaway (The Black Widow Bride)
**Status**: Deceased
**Occupation**: Serial killer
**Notable Crime**: Multiple husband murders

Constance Hatchaway, known as "The Black Widow Bride," was a serial killer who married wealthy men and murdered them to inherit their estates. Her last known kill was in 1877 when she married George Hightower. After moving to New Orleans, she murdered him with an axe and added his head to her collection.

**Connections**:
- Married to George Hightower (1877)
- Connected to the Hightower family through marriage

**Locations**: Haunted Mansion (Disneyland, Magic Kingdom, Tokyo Disneyland)

---

### Henry Ravenswood
**Status**: Deceased (1860), spirit active
**Occupation**: Mining magnate, founder of Thunder Mesa
**Also Known As**: The Phantom

Henry Ravenswood (born 1795) struck gold in Big Thunder Mountain and founded the Thunder Mesa Mining Company and the city of Thunder Mesa. He built a Victorian manor on Boot Hill overlooking the mountain. In the Disneyland Paris version, he personally oversaw Barnabas T. Bullion's mining operations. In 1860, an earthquake killed the Ravenswood family. Henry's spirit became the Phantom of Phantom Manor, vowing that no one would marry his daughter Melanie, murdering multiple suitors.

**Connections**:
- Oversaw Barnabas T. Bullion's Big Thunder mining operations (Paris version)
- Father of Melanie Ravenswood
- Husband of Martha Ravenswood

**Locations**: Phantom Manor (Disneyland Paris), Big Thunder Mountain Railroad lore

---

### Melanie Ravenswood
**Status**: Deceased (1860), spirit active
**Born**: 1842
**Parents**: Henry and Martha Ravenswood

Melanie Ravenswood was the daughter of Henry Ravenswood. She had multiple suitors (Barry Claude, Sawyer Bottom, Captain Rowan D. Falls, and Ignatius "Iggy" Knight) who were all killed in various "accidents" staged by her father's ghost. She remains in Phantom Manor in her bridal gown, with a faint figure of her father standing behind her in her portrait.

**Connections**:
- Daughter of Henry Ravenswood
- Victim of her father's supernatural interference

**Locations**: Phantom Manor (Disneyland Paris)

---

### Madame Zarkov
**Status**: Active (historical period)
**Occupation**: Proprietress of Museum of the Weird
**Origin**: Romani

Madame Zarkov was a Romani woman who worked as proprietress of the Museum of the Weird, an establishment holding a collection of arcane and supernatural relics. Jason Chandler consulted with her about supernatural occurrences at Big Thunder Mountain. She warned Barnabas T. Bullion to stop mining operations due to paranormal dangers, but he ignored her warnings.

**Connections**:
- Consulted by Jason Chandler
- Warned Barnabas T. Bullion about the Thunderbird curse

**Locations**: Museum of the Weird (unbuilt), referenced at Big Thunder Mountain Railroad (Magic Kingdom)

## Legendary Figures Referenced

### Leonardo da Vinci
While not a direct character in the S.E.A. lore, Leonardo da Vinci is referenced as an iconic historical figure associated with the society's founding era. At Fortress Explorations, "The Leonardo Challenge" scavenger hunt features an actor playing da Vinci's ghost. Camellia Falco's Dream Flyer was inspired by his flying machine designs.

**Locations**: Fortress Explorations (Tokyo DisneySea), Soaring: Fantastic Flight inspiration
