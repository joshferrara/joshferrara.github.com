# Significant Artifacts & Objects

A catalog of notable artifacts, relics, and objects within the S.E.A. universe.

## Cursed & Supernatural Artifacts

### Shiriki Utundu
**Type**: Cursed wooden idol
**Origin**: Mtundu tribe, Congo Basin, Africa
**Current Status**: Housed in Hotel Hightower (presumably still there after Hightower's disappearance)
**Possessor**: Harrison Hightower III (1899 - disappeared)

**Description**: A wooden idol of African origin depicting a figure holding a dagger and spear with a small skull adorning its chest. The name translates to "Believe the Misfortune."

**Legend**: The idol is possessed by the spirit of an old shaman. The Mtundu tribe stole the idol from another wealthy tribe about 60-70 years before 1899.

**Rules for Possession**:
- Always treat it with respect
- Do not have it close to fire
- Must not be wrapped up, buried, or confined in a small building
- Must never be thrown or given away
- **Beware its eyes**

**Curse**: Harrison Hightower III extinguished his cigar on the idol's head on December 31, 1899, mocking the curse. At midnight, green lightning struck the hotel, and Hightower disappeared in the elevator.

**Location**: Tower of Terror (Tokyo DisneySea)

---

### The Enchanted Music Box
**Type**: Magical artifact
**Origin**: Unknown
**Possessor**: Lord Henry Mystic
**Current Status**: Active at Mystic Manor

**Description**: An ornate music box with magical properties that brings inanimate objects to life.

**Event**: Albert (Lord Henry Mystic's monkey) opened the music box, causing all artifacts in Mystic Manor to come alive and creating chaos throughout the manor.

**Location**: Mystic Manor (Hong Kong Disneyland)

---

### Water God Idol Head (Ah-Kutlikutli)
**Type**: Sacred artifact / temple component
**Origin**: 5,000-year-old temple in Lost River Delta
**Possessor**: Stolen by Harrison Hightower III and his valet
**Current Status**: Unknown

**Description**: The head of the water god (Ah-Kutlikutli) statue from an ancient temple featuring two deities.

**Curse**: Stealing the head angered the water god. Later, the Hightower Trust accidentally positioned the fire god (Ixchulacoatl) and water god to face each other, causing both deities to battle and create supernatural destruction at the archaeological site.

**Location**: Raging Spirits (Tokyo DisneySea)

---

### The Emerald Trinity
**Type**: Mysterious artifact (limited details available)
**Pursuer**: Professor Garrett Reed (after S.E.A. expulsion)
**Current Status**: Unknown

**Description**: An artifact sought by Professor Garrett Reed following his expulsion from S.E.A. for using questionable tactics.

**Location**: Jungle Cruise - Curse of the Emerald Trinity (Hong Kong Disneyland)

### The Crystal Skull
**Type**: Mysterious artifact connected to Fountain of Youth
**Origin**: Temple of the Crystal Skull, Lost River Delta
**Current Status**: Remains in temple

**Description**: Unlike the Disneyland version's Mara deity, Tokyo DisneySea's Indiana Jones Adventure centers on this Crystal Skull. Serves a similar mystical role to Mara in the Temple of the Forbidden Eye.

**Connection**: Harrison Hightower III photographed outside this temple.

**Location**: Indiana Jones Adventure: Temple of the Crystal Skull (Tokyo DisneySea)

---

### Mara (Deity/Entity)
**Type**: Ancient deity
**Origin**: Temple of the Forbidden Eye, India (banks of Bengali river)
**Current Status**: Active in temple

**Description**: The deity Mara offers three gifts to temple visitors:

## Vehicles & Transportation

### The Renaissance
**Type**: Sailing ship / galleon
**Origin**: Porto Paradiso, Italy
**Owner**: Society of Explorers and Adventurers
**Current Status**: Docked at Explorers Landing

**Description**: The sailing ship belonging to S.E.A., docked at the society's original headquarters. Guests can explore the decks and cabins, imagining themselves as crew members.

**Historical Significance**: Associated with S.E.A. since the society's founding in 1538.

**Location**: Fortress Explorations (Tokyo DisneySea)

---

### The Dream Flyer
**Type**: Glider / flying machine
**Designer**: Camellia Falco
**Inspiration**: Leonardo da Vinci's flying machine designs
**Current Status**: Museum exhibit / operational (magical)

**Description**: A glider developed by aviation pioneer Camellia Falco. The spirit of Falco guides guests on flights around the world using this device.

**Historical Significance**: Represents the first female S.E.A. member's major invention, inspired by da Vinci's Renaissance-era concepts.

**Location**: Soaring: Fantastic Flight / Museum of Fantastic Flight (Tokyo DisneySea)

---

### The Hyperion (Airship)

**Era**: Post-1907
