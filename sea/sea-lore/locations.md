# Locations & Attractions

A comprehensive guide to all Disney park locations featuring S.E.A. lore and references.

## Tokyo DisneySea (Japan)

### Fortress Explorations
**Area**: Mediterranean Harbor
**Opening**: September 4, 2001
**Type**: Walk-through attraction

The headquarters of the Society of Explorers and Adventurers, established August 12, 1538, at Explorers Landing in Porto Paradiso, Italy.

**Features**:
- Foucault pendulum
- Astral observatory
- The Renaissance sailing ship (docked at Explorers Landing)
- Flying machines inspired by Leonardo da Vinci
- Alchemy laboratory
- Navigation center
- Optical illusion room
- Hall of Explorers honoring historical figures (Leonardo da Vinci, Ferdinand Magellan, Marco Polo, Christopher Columbus)

**S.E.A. Connection**: Original headquarters of the society, founding location

**Special Program**: "The Leonardo Challenge" scavenger hunt (started July 7, 2008) featuring an actor playing Leonardo da Vinci's ghost on interactive kiosks

---

### Tower of Terror
**Area**: American Waterfront
**Opening**: September 22, 2006
**Type**: Drop tower thrill ride

The Hotel Hightower in New York City, owned by corrupt S.E.A. member Harrison Hightower III.

**Story**: In 1899, Harrison Hightower III stole the cursed idol Shiriki Utundu from the Mtundu tribe in the Congo Basin. On December 31, 1899, during a New Year's Eve party, Hightower mocked the curse by extinguishing his cigar on the idol's head. At midnight, green lightning struck, and Hightower disappeared in the elevator amid screams.

**S.E.A. Characters**: Harrison Hightower III, his valet Smelding

**Connections to Other Attractions**:
- Photo shows Hightower outside Temple of the Crystal Skull (Indiana Jones Adventure)
- Mural depicts Hightower stealing from Raging Spirits temple
- Hightower Trust crates appear in Raging Spirits queue
- References to Expedition Everest appear in Skipper Canteen books about Hightower

**Artifacts**: Shiriki Utundu (cursed idol)

---

### Raging Spirits
**Area**: Lost River Delta
**Type**: Roller coaster

An archaeological site featuring a 5,000-year-old temple with two altars: one for fire god Ixchulacoatl and one for water god Ah-Kutlikutli.

**Story**: Explorers (including Harrison Hightower and his valet) stole the head from the water god statue. The Hightower Trust later accidentally restored the temple so the fire and water gods faced each other, causing the deities to become enraged and create mass destruction.

**S.E.A. Connection**: Harrison Hightower III and the Hightower Trust

**Adjacent Attraction**: Indiana Jones Adventure: Temple of the Crystal Skull (separate but nearby in Lost River Delta)

---

### Indiana Jones Adventure: Temple of the Crystal Skull
**Area**: Lost River Delta
**Opening**: September 4, 2001
**Type**: Enhanced motion vehicle dark ride

A temple in the Lost River Delta (distinct from the Disneyland version's Temple of the Forbidden Eye).

**Story**: Unlike the Disneyland version centered on deity Mara and the Forbidden Eye, this version focuses on the Crystal Skull and the search for the Fountain of Youth.

**S.E.A. Connection**: Harrison Hightower III photographed outside this temple; Jock Lindsay (S.E.A. member) as Indiana Jones' pilot

---

### Soaring: Fantastic Flight
**Area**: Mediterranean Harbor
**Opening**: July 23, 2019
**Type**: Flying theater simulator

The Museum of Fantastic Flight in Porto Paradiso, presenting a retrospective on Camellia Falco.

**Story**: Camellia Falco was an aviation pioneer and the first female S.E.A. member (inducted 1851). She developed the Dream Flyer, a glider inspired by Leonardo da Vinci's designs. Her spirit guides guests on a flight around the world.

**S.E.A. Characters**: Camellia Falco

**Special Features**: Exclusive Tokyo scene; laser IMAX projectors; wind, sound, and scent effects

**Connection**: Inspired by Leonardo da Vinci's flying machine designs

---

## Hong Kong Disneyland

### Mystic Manor
**Area**: Mystic Point
**Opening**: May 17, 2013
**Type**: Trackless dark ride

Lord Henry Mystic's manor housing his extensive artifact collection.

**Story**: Lord Henry Mystic rescued his monkey companion Albert from a giant spider. At Mystic Manor, Albert opens an enchanted music box that brings the entire collection to life.

**S.E.A. Characters**: Lord Henry Mystic, Albert (monkey)

**Connections**:
- Photograph shows Mystic with Harrison Hightower III and Shiriki Utundu
- Referenced in books at Skipper Canteen
- Portrait appears in Disney Cruise Line Oceaneer Lab
- Dr. Kon Chunosuke received crate of books from Mystic Point

**Special Features**: Trackless ride system; musical score by Danny Elfman

**Historic Significance**: Harrison Hightower III was retroactively established as S.E.A. member with this attraction's opening (2013)

---

### Jungle Cruise - Curse of the Emerald Trinity
**Area**: Adventureland
**Type**: Boat ride overlay

Special overlay featuring Professor Garrett Reed.

**Story**: Professor Garrett Reed, expelled from S.E.A. by Lord Henry Mystic for questionable tactics, seeks the Emerald Trinity.

**S.E.A. Characters**: Professor Garrett Reed (former member)

## Walt Disney World (Florida)

### Magic Kingdom

#### Jungle Skipper Canteen
**Area**: Adventureland
**Opening**: December 16, 2015
**Type**: Table service restaurant

Founded by Dr. Albert Falls; currently operated through the legacy of the Jungle Navigation Company.

**S.E.A. Connection**: Meeting room features walls with bookshelves filled with books written by S.E.A. members

**S.E.A. Members Referenced**:
- Dr. Albert Falls (founder)
- Chef Tandaji (cookbook: "Great Recipes of Africa")
- Sango Sio (confirmed via fez)
- Luana Teixeira (photographer, confirmed via fez)
- Merriweather Adam Pleasure (fez confirms S.E.A. membership)
- Harrison Hightower III (books referencing Expedition Everest)
- Multiple other members through artifacts and books

---

#### Big Thunder Mountain Railroad
**Area**: Frontierland
**Type**: Mine train roller coaster

The Big Thunder Mining Company, established 1848 by Barnabas T. Bullion.

**Story**: Barnabas T. Bullion saw it as his destiny to acquire all gold from Big Thunder Mountain. His friend Jason Chandler (S.E.A. president) contacted Madame Zarkov of the Museum of the Weird, who warned Bullion to stop operations due to the Thunderbird curse. Bullion ignored the warnings.

**S.E.A. Characters**: Barnabas T. Bullion, Jason Chandler, Madame Zarkov (Museum of the Weird)

**Easter Egg**: Queue contains hidden Museum of the Weird reference

---

#### Jungle Cruise
**Area**: Adventureland
**Opening**: 1971 (2021 major update)
**Type**: Boat ride

The Jungle Navigation Company, founded by Dr. Albert Falls, now operated by his granddaughter Alberta Falls.

**Story (2021 Update)**: Alberta Falls inherited the company after Dr. Albert Falls' mysterious disappearance. In 1938, she met Dr. Kon Chunosuke, recognizing the S.E.A. logo on his equipment. Their expedition with skipper Felix Pechman XIII went wrong when the boat crashed, stranding them in the African veldt where they were chased by a rhinoceros.

**S.E.A. Characters**:
- Dr. Albert Falls (founder, missing)
- Alberta Falls (granddaughter, current owner)
- Dr. Kon Chunosuke (1938 expedition)
- Felix Pechman XIII (skipper)
- Siobhan "Puffin" Murphy (Alberta's distant cousin)

**New Characters (2021)**:
- Dr. Leonard Moss (Canadian botanist)
- Rosa Soto Dominguez (Mexican artist)

---

### Typhoon Lagoon

#### Miss Adventure Falls
**Area**: Typhoon Lagoon water park
**Opening**: March 12, 2017
**Type**: Family raft ride

Captain Mary Oceaneer's treasure collection, scattered by a rogue typhoon.

**Story**: Captain Mary Oceaneer, world-famous treasure hunter and S.E.A. member, sailed the seven seas collecting treasures. While diving, a rogue storm struck, carrying her ship and artifacts to Typhoon Lagoon. Guests journey through her scattered treasure collection.

**S.E.A. Characters**: Captain Mary Oceaneer, Duncan (parrot, diving partner)

**Connections**: Captain Mary's artifacts also appear in Disney Cruise Line Oceaneer Labs

**Artifacts**:
- Golden sword (discovered December 5, 1901)
- Diving suit for her parrot Salty
- Various treasures from around the world

---

### Disney Springs

#### Jock Lindsey's Hangar Bar
**Area**: The Landing, Disney Springs
**Opening**: September 22, 2015
**Type**: Bar and restaurant

Jock Lindsay's airplane hangar, air tower, and waterfront property.

**Story**: In 1938, Jock Lindsay (Indiana Jones' pilot) and Indy discovered Disney Springs while chasing a mythology tip. Jock bought waterfront property and settled down, piloting seaplane tours across Florida. He invited fellow S.E.A. members to visit.

**S.E.A. Characters**: Jock Lindsay

**Connections**:
- Indiana Jones universe
- Packed with artifacts referencing S.E.A. universe
- References to other S.E.A. members

**Themed Areas**: Vintage aviation ambiance with spare airplane parts, salvaged equipment, and adventure mementos

**Area**: Adventureland
**Opening**: December 19, 2018
**Type**: Quick-service location

**S.E.A. Connection**: Features a wall of expedition paddles left behind by S.E.A. members; commemorates several society members

**Characters**: Rosita (parrot "entertainer" from Enchanted Tiki Room)

### Haunted Mansion
**Area**: New Orleans Square
**Type**: Dark ride attraction

The Haunted Mansion in New Orleans, Louisiana (in the lore).

**S.E.A. Connection**: In 1877, Constance Hatchaway (the Black Widow Bride) married George Hightower and moved to this mansion. She murdered him and inherited the estate, connecting to the Hightower family and Tower of Terror lore.

**Characters**: Constance Hatchaway, George Hightower

## Disneyland Paris (France)

### Phantom Manor
**Area**: Frontierland (Thunder Mesa)
**Type**: Dark ride attraction

The Ravenswood Manor on Boot Hill in Thunder Mesa, overlooking Big Thunder Mountain.

**Story**: Henry Ravenswood (born 1795) struck gold and founded Thunder Mesa. He built a manor for his family. In the Paris version, he oversaw Barnabas T. Bullion's mining operations. An 1860 earthquake killed the Ravenswood family. Henry's spirit became The Phantom, murdering all of his daughter Melanie's suitors.

**Characters**:
- Henry Ravenswood / The Phantom
- Melanie Ravenswood
- Martha Ravenswood
- Melanie's murdered suitors: Barry Claude, Sawyer Bottom, Captain Rowan D. Falls, Ignatius "Iggy" Knight

**Connection**: Henry oversaw Big Thunder mining operations in this version

**2019 Refurbishment**: Confirmed The Phantom is Henry Ravenswood

---

### Big Thunder Mountain
**Area**: Frontierland
**Type**: Mine train roller coaster

Thunder Mesa mining town, controlled by Henry Ravenswood who oversaw operations for Barnabas T. Bullion.

**Paris Version Unique Story**: In 1848, Bullion received land grant; Henry Ravenswood personally oversaw the mines. 1860 earthquake destroyed part of town and killed the Ravenswoods.

**S.E.A. Connection**: Links Henry Ravenswood to Barnabas T. Bullion and the broader Big Thunder mythology

## Disneyland (California) - Additional Locations

### Indiana Jones Adventure: Temple of the Forbidden Eye
**Area**: Adventureland
**Opening**: March 3, 1995
**Type**: Enhanced motion vehicle dark ride

The Temple of the Forbidden Eye in India on the banks of a Bengali river in the Lost Delta.

**Story**: The temple honors deity Mara, who offers three gifts (eternal youth, earthly riches, visions of future) on condition visitors never gaze into Mara's eyes.

**S.E.A. Connection**: Jock Lindsay as Indiana Jones' pilot; Harrison Hightower III photographed at Crystal Skull temple (Tokyo version)

**Note**: Different from Tokyo's Crystal Skull version but part of shared Indy universe

## Disney's Animal Kingdom (Florida)

### Expedition Everest
**Area**: Asia
**Opening**: April 7, 2006
**Type**: Steel roller coaster

The Forbidden Mountain in the village of Serka Zong, Nepal, near Mount Everest.

**Story**: Expedition operators Bob and Norbu route guests through the Forbidden Mountain despite warnings from villagers about the protective Yeti spirit that guards the Himalayas.

**S.E.A. Connection**: Harrison Hightower III wrote books referencing this expedition (books appear at Skipper Canteen)

**Mythology**: Distinct village of Serka Zong with local belief in protective Yeti spirit

## Unmaterialized / Referenced Locations

### Museum of the Weird
**Status**: Unbuilt attraction (planned as Haunted Mansion extension)
**Designer**: Rolly Crump

Museum holding arcane and supernatural relics.

**Proprietress**: Madame Zarkov (Romani woman)

**S.E.A. Connection**: Jason Chandler consulted with Madame Zarkov about Big Thunder Mountain supernatural occurrences. She warned Barnabas T. Bullion about the curse.

**References**: Easter egg in Big Thunder Mountain Railroad queue (Magic Kingdom)

---

### Discovery Bay
**Status**: Unbuilt Disneyland land concept

Original concept for an area featuring inventors and explorers.

**S.E.A. Connection**: Jason Chandler was created for this concept and later incorporated into S.E.A. lore

**Legacy**: Elements incorporated into various S.E.A. attractions worldwide
